A Bit About Me

I am a recent graduate from the University of Guelph where I earned my Bachelor of Computer Science Honours with a minor in Geography. Throughout my time at Guelph I learned everything from how to use tunneling to increase network security, to creating a neural network to classify hand written numbers from 0 - 9. Additionally, I was part of a project that researched new ways to indentify flood risk in New Orleans using social and physical factors.

On a more personal note, I enjoy focusing on improving my hobbies. I love to mountain bike, boulder and cook. I have worked at multiple restaurants which ignited my interest in creating expressive, tasty and beautiful dishes I can enjoy with friends and family.

Some of my Projects

A neural network that uses forward and backwards propagation learning to classify images of handwritten number into their respective categories.

An image editor that can perform a series of transformation and manipulations upon given images.

A New Orleans flood risk map series developed with socioeconomic and physical risk factors using GIS tools and software.
